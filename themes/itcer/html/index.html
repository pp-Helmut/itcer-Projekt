ITCER e. V. and ITCER Kenya being non-profit organizations,founded in Jan. 2017 and Jan. 2018, resp.,

  • to offer high quality training in environmental science based on ongoing research use cases for postgraduate students (MSc and PhD levels) from Kenya and neighbouring countries
  • to act as an outreach centre for providing concepts and materials as starting points for implementing up-to-date environmental science knowledge in tertiary institutions

ITCER e. V.

A non-profit association according to the German law (‚Vereinsrecht‘)
Seat of the association in Bayreuth, Germany
Registered by Amtsgericht Bayreuth in July, 2016

ITCER Kenya

A non-profit NGO according to the Kenyan law
Seat of the association in Siaya, Kenya
Registered by NGO Coordination Board Nairobi in September, 2017

Educational Concept and Planning

The envisaged courses offer training and skills in the field of

  • Vegetation science and Eco(physio)logy
  • Plant pathology and Parasitology
  • Microbiology and Mycology
  • Soil science and Hydrology
  • Biological informatics and Remote sensing

Accreditation of concept and content of the ITCER courses by the Commission for University Education (CUE), Nairobi, Kenya

Lecturers from Europe contributing with their expertise during a period of 2 weeks

Up to 15 students per course

Management committee comprising Kenyan and German members

  • Evaluation of course applicants, management of correspondence, and student admission to the courses
  • Local administrator involved in management and registration formalities.

Students will receive lecture materials and practical exercise protocols.

Participants to get certificates showing credit points for the accredited courses taken

Location of training center

Located in Siaya County in Western Kenya near Lake Victoria.

Location characterised by

  • developed public infrastructure
  • accessibility from airports in Nairobi and Kisumu
  • proximity to diverse natural ecosystems such as savanna, rainforest, and wetlands that provide training grounds for students
